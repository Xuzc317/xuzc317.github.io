Research Project 2026.01 — Present
System Reconstruction under Distribution Shift: A Case Study in Child Speech Emotion Recognition

Reproduced a published model reporting 86.82% accuracy. Discovered 63% speaker overlap between train and test sets — systemic data leakage inflating all reported results. After enforcing strict speaker-independent partitioning (MD5 hash, zero-leakage assertion), accuracy collapsed to ~35%, near chance. Root cause: mean-pooled handcrafted features (162-dim) destroyed temporal structure, making Conv1d slide on the feature-concatenation axis rather than time — the architecture was mathematically incapable of temporal modeling.

Rebuilt a distribution-driven three-module framework (Data Pipeline → WavLM Backbone → Temporal Pooling). Adopted WavLM Base+ frame-level SSL features (768-dim × T) with learnable 12-layer fusion weights. Designed Prosody-guided Temporal Importance Pooling — using children's F0 and energy contours to weight frames, replacing blind mean pooling. Quantified distribution shift via Fréchet Distance (FD) and SMMD, establishing FD ∝ 1/Accuracy as a predictive relationship.

92.78% test WA on C-BESD under strict 6:2:2 speaker-independent protocol (Self-Attention pooling). Prosody Pooling achieves 91.30% WA with stronger interpretability (APC=0.718). Cross-corpus experiments reveal distribution shift boundaries: IEMOCAP (FD=7.20, 58.67% WA), FAU Aibo (FD=8.50, 66.36% WA in-domain, 19.56% zero-shot). Multi-seed robustness verified (65.15%±1.12 on FAU). Manuscript submitted.

Research Project 2026.05 — 2026.06
SMDSL: A Domain-Specific Language for Embodied Intelligence Spatial-Motion Modeling

Designed and prototyped a domain-specific language (DSL) for describing spatial configurations and motion primitives in embodied AI scenarios. The DSL provides a structured, composable syntax to specify 3D spatial relationships, robotic motion trajectories, and action sequences — bridging the gap between high-level task descriptions and low-level robot control commands.

Built a parsing and execution demo that translates DSL scripts into actionable robot instructions. Explored the design space of spatial-action abstraction layers, focusing on expressiveness, composability, and human readability. The DSL targets complex embodied tasks where spatial reasoning and motion planning are tightly coupled.
